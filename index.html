About Prof. Mohamed Ali Khalil

    Theme of main research

    My research focuses on the detection and classification aspect of non-stationary signals. The objective is to propose adaptive detection algorithms in the case of lack of knowledge of the parameters of the events to be detected in uterine EMG signals.

    One of the variants consists in using the wavelet-type multi-resolution approaches in the sequential detection phases. Fast algorithms have also been proposed.

    This research work is currently reinforced thanks to the many co-supervised theses, particularly in the context of collaboration with UTC. A comparative study will soon be carried out, requiring acquisition campaigns, both in France and in Lebanon. The goal is to model and predict the threats of preterm birth.

    In the same context of biomedical research, I am interested in the study and modeling of other types of signals such as ECG, in collaboration with the University Paris-Cr√©teil (UPEC) (Prof. Amine Nait-Ali), especially on biometric applications.

    Teaching engineering level

    My participation in teaching in engineering education includes several courses. Several modules were created by myself (PowerPoint support) in Lebanon, and I prepared the TPs for all these modules.

    Pattern Recognition (21H Courses + TD)

    This course covers parametric classification (likelihood ratio, Bayes criteria with and without rejection), nonparametric classification (Parzen and KPPV methods), and principal component analysis. Neural networks: Adaline, Delta, Perceptron, backpropagation, radial function, competitive networks. TPs are created using MATLAB or Python.

    Microprocessors (42H Courses + TD)

    Designed for students in biomedical, computer, and telecommunications specializations. It introduces logic integrated circuits and microprocessor architecture, including assembly programming and I/O management. Full support material for courses, TD, and TP was prepared.

    Microcontrollers and Interfaces (42H Courses + TD)

    For computer and biomedical students. Focused on the PIC microcontroller (16F84, 16F877), including LCD, stepper motor, keyboard, graphic LCD, PLC interfacing. I created a specialized lab and introduced DSP concepts.

    Biomedical Electronics ‚Äì Sensors and Instrumentation (84H Courses + TD + TP)

    Aimed at biomedical and electronics students. Covers instrumentation design, biomedical signal acquisition, processing, and transmission. Includes medical imaging (Ultrasound, MRI, X-ray, CT), and devices such as respirators, photometers, spectrophotometers, hemodialysis, and hematology tools. MATLAB TPs included.

    Instrumentation (32H Courses + Tutorials)

    Introduces acquisition chain elements (conditioner, amplifier, multiplexer, ADC/DAC) and microprocessor interfaces. Includes hands-on LABVIEW TPs with National Instruments acquisition boards.

    Digital Signal Processing (42H Courses + TD)

    Covers numerical correlation, convolution, linear systems, discrete and fast Fourier transforms, and digital filter design (FIR, IIR). Includes practical MATLAB sessions which I also implemented.

    Teaching master

    I taught the following M2R courses:

    Probability and Statistics (12 H Courses + TD UL)

    This course, taught in mechanical DEA at the Lebanese University, recalls theories of probability, reliability, statistical laws, and hypothesis testing. An introduction is also provided on the likelihood ratio and its use for detection and classification in signals.

    Time Frequencies and Wavelets (Master UTC-UIL and Master Le Havre)

    This course covers frequency-time concepts, such as windowed Fourier transform, Wigner-Ville distribution, and other bilinear time-frequency representations. Additionally, it covers time scales, the wavelet transform, subband decomposition, multi-resolution analysis, and the types of wavelets. TPs are conducted under Matlab.

    Image Processing (28 hours of course + TD)

    This course is designed for students in the biomedical sector, aiming to familiarize them with techniques, equipment, and medical image processing. Topics include acquisition, visualization, enhancement and correction, image compression, and image transfer in the biomedical context. TP was created for this course.

    Advanced Signal Processing (32H Course + TD)

    This advanced course in signal processing covers AutoRegressive Modeling (AR), Moving Average (MA), ARMA, Time Frequency, Time Scale (wavelets), estimation and parameterization, detection, and classification in signals. It also introduces form recognition. The module was created at the Lebanese University and ESIGELEC in Rouen, France, along with its TP.

    Computer Vision (25 Hours) (Master in Biometry, France, English)

    This advanced course focuses on computer vision techniques, such as image segmentation, stereo vision, stereo construction, camera calibration, stereo calibration, correspondence, image recognition, and convolutional neural networks.

    Machine Learning and Deep Learning (32 hours)

    This course provides an introduction to pattern recognition and neural networks, covering topics such as Adaline, Delta, Perceptron, backpropagation, radial functions, competitive networks, and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and their families. It also covers semantic segmentation and classification with Python Labs.
